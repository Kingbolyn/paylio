# PAYLIO VISUAL DIRECTION SHEET 05

# Money Transfer

### Money in Motion

---

## Purpose

The transfer experience is where Paylio demonstrates trust.

Every interaction should reassure the user that their money is moving exactly as intended.

The objective is not speed alone.

The objective is confidence.

Users should never wonder:

"Did it go through?"

The interface should answer that question before they ask it.

---

## Emotional Objective

The transfer journey should create five emotions.

Clarity.

Confidence.

Focus.

Control.

Relief.

Each step should naturally lead into the next.

Nothing should feel rushed.

Nothing should create uncertainty.

---

## Story

Imagine handing something valuable to someone you trust.

You make eye contact.

They acknowledge receiving it.

You both understand the exchange is complete.

No confusion.

No hesitation.

No anxiety.

That human feeling should exist digitally.

Sending money should feel calm.

---

## Design Philosophy

The transfer experience is a guided conversation.

Never a complicated form.

Never a crowded page.

Every screen has one purpose.

Every decision feels obvious.

The interface should quietly guide the user.

Never overwhelm them.

---

## Flow

Step One.

Choose who receives the money.

Step Two.

Choose how much.

Step Three.

Review everything carefully.

Step Four.

Authenticate.

Step Five.

Receive confirmation.

Each screen focuses on only one decision.

Never ask multiple important questions at once.

---

## Recipient Selection

The contact list should feel personal.

Clean profile images.

Elegant typography.

Comfortable spacing.

Intelligent search.

Recent recipients appear naturally.

Frequently used contacts feel familiar.

Nothing feels like a database.

It feels like people.

---

## Amount Entry

The amount screen should be almost empty.

The number becomes the hero.

Large typography.

Beautiful spacing.

The keypad feels premium.

Every digit responds naturally.

The user should feel the value of the amount they are entering.

Not because of flashy effects.

Because of thoughtful presentation.

---

## Review Screen

This is the moment of confidence.

Everything slows down.

The recipient.

The amount.

The note.

The transfer fee.

The remaining balance.

Everything is beautifully organized.

Nothing hidden.

Nothing rushed.

The interface quietly asks:

"Is this exactly what you want?"

---

## Authentication

Security should never interrupt trust.

Entering the PIN should feel reassuring.

Not stressful.

The keypad is elegant.

Each entered digit softly responds.

Biometric authentication feels effortless.

The scanner animation is calm.

Everything communicates protection.

Never fear.

---

## Confirmation

The confirmation moment defines the memory.

Money moves.

The balance updates.

The balance card softly illuminates.

The transaction appears.

The insights update.

A calm confirmation animation appears.

Not fireworks.

Not confetti.

Simply beautiful certainty.

The interface quietly says:

**Money sent.**

Nothing more is necessary.

---

## Information Hierarchy

Recipient.

↓

Amount.

↓

Confirmation.

↓

Authentication.

↓

Completion.

The user should never question where they are in the process.

Progress feels obvious.

---

## Lighting

Soft teal illumination grows stronger as the transfer progresses.

The final confirmation creates the brightest moment.

The glow gently fades.

Everything returns to calm.

Light becomes emotional feedback.

---

## Materials

Deep glass.

Soft matte surfaces.

Premium buttons.

Elegant typography.

Subtle reflections.

Everything feels crafted.

Nothing resembles a web form.

---

## Motion Philosophy

Every transition feels continuous.

Pages slide naturally.

Cards gently expand.

Numbers count.

Confirmation ripples.

Buttons respond immediately.

Nothing pops.

Nothing bounces.

Nothing surprises the user.

Motion exists to reduce uncertainty.

---

## Atmosphere

The transfer experience should feel like being inside a quiet financial studio.

No distractions.

No unnecessary graphics.

Only thoughtful guidance.

Every interaction communicates confidence.

---

## Sound (Future Direction)

Button touches produce soft tactile feedback.

PIN entry feels precise.

Transfer completion creates a warm glass confirmation tone.

Receiving money produces a gentle harmonic chime.

No harsh sounds.

No loud notifications.

---

## Cinematography

Imagine filming this experience with an ARRI Alexa 65.

The camera slowly follows the user's journey.

Light shifts gently as progress is made.

The balance card becomes brighter after completion.

The confirmation animation feels almost physical.

Everything is beautifully paced.

---

## Things to Avoid

Do not create long forms.

Do not overload the screen with fields.

Do not interrupt users with unnecessary pop-ups.

Do not use bright success banners.

Do not animate excessively.

Do not celebrate sending money.

Respect the seriousness of financial transactions.

Confidence is more powerful than excitement.

---

## Quality Standard

The transfer experience should become one of the smoothest interactions in the entire product.

Every transition should feel invisible.

Every confirmation should build trust.

Every animation should reassure.

Users should finish the transfer feeling completely certain that everything happened exactly as expected.

## Design Principle for Claude Code

A transfer is not just a transaction.

It is a promise between the user and the product.

Every design decision should reinforce that promise.

When the transfer is complete, the user should not feel excitement. They should feel certainty.

The lasting impression should be:

**"I never had to wonder whether my money arrived. Paylio made me feel confident every step of the way."**
