# PAYLIO VISUAL DIRECTION SHEET 14

# Mobile Experience

### Pocket-Sized Luxury

---

## Purpose

The mobile experience is where Paylio lives.

Most users will never open a desktop browser.

Their entire relationship with Paylio happens within a rectangle they carry everywhere.

The product must feel extraordinary in that space.

Not adapted.

Not compressed.

Designed for it from the beginning.

---

## Emotional Objective

The mobile experience should create:

Effortlessness.

Confidence.

Intimacy.

Trust.

Calm.

The product should feel built around the user's hand.

Not around a screen size.

---

## Story

Imagine the most valuable thing currently in your pocket.

It is small.

It is powerful.

It communicates its quality through every interaction.

You reach for it without thinking.

It responds before you expect it to.

It never disappoints.

Paylio should feel like that object.

The most considered financial experience you carry.

---

## Design Philosophy

Mobile design is not about reduction.

It is about focus.

Every screen must do one thing beautifully.

Every gesture must feel natural.

Every touch must feel immediate.

The product should disappear into the hand.

---

## The Screen

430 pixels maximum width.

Every component designed for this space.

Nothing adapted from desktop.

Nothing squeezed.

Generous vertical space.

Breathing room between every element.

Mobile deserves the same spatial generosity as any other format.

---

## Touch Targets

Every interactive element minimum 44 pixels tall.

Minimum 44 pixels wide.

Never smaller.

Users should never miss a target.

Frustration should never enter the experience.

Confidence should.

---

## Thumb Navigation

The most important interactions belong at the bottom.

Not the top.

Bottom navigation.

Bottom action sheets.

Bottom confirm buttons.

Paylio should be entirely comfortable with one hand.

The thumb should never stretch.

---

## Scrolling

Scrolling should feel effortless.

Natural momentum.

No resistance.

No snap where snap is not deserved.

The content should feel lighter than gravity.

Users should enjoy scrolling.

Not endure it.

---

## The Balance Card

On mobile, the balance card fills the primary visual zone.

Large.

Dominant.

The entire screen exists to support it.

Scrolling reveals supporting information below.

The card never shrinks to accommodate content.

Content accommodates the card.

---

## Quick Actions

Quick actions should feel like premium buttons.

Large enough to press with confidence.

Close enough together to reach naturally.

Spaced with intention.

Never crowded.

Four actions maximum in a horizontal row.

---

## Bottom Navigation

Fixed.

Floating.

Beautifully blurred.

Five destinations.

Icons only.

Labels below.

Active state indicated by teal icon and top border accent.

The navigation bar should feel like part of the device.

Not part of the interface.

---

## Typography on Mobile

Balance figures at 44 pixels minimum.

Section headers at 20 pixels.

Body text at 15 pixels.

Labels at 11 pixels with generous tracking.

Nothing below 11 pixels.

Every line of text deserves legibility.

---

## Loading

Loading should never feel like waiting.

Skeleton states appear immediately.

They breathe.

They suggest the content to come.

When content arrives,

it replaces skeletons naturally.

Without flash.

Without jump.

The transition should be invisible.

---

## Gestures

Swipe to navigate back.

Swipe to dismiss bottom sheets.

Long press for contextual options.

Pull to refresh where context demands it.

Every gesture should feel predictable.

Nothing experimental.

Nothing requiring discovery.

---

## Haptics

Every significant interaction deserves subtle haptic feedback.

PIN digit entry.

Transfer confirmation.

Authentication success.

Card freeze toggle.

Haptics communicate without sound.

They make the interface feel physical.

---

## Safe Areas

All content respects device safe areas.

Top notch.

Bottom home indicator.

No content disappears beneath them.

No interaction is blocked by them.

The product should feel designed for the specific device.

Not simply rendered on it.

---

## Atmosphere

The dark navy environment feels intentional on mobile.

OLED screens render deep navy as true black.

Light appears to emerge from darkness.

The balance card glows.

The ambient particles drift.

The experience becomes immersive.

Not just functional.

---

## Cinematography

Imagine holding the most beautiful phone on earth.

And opening the most beautiful banking application ever made.

The screen fills your peripheral vision.

The balance occupies the top third.

Everything is precisely positioned.

Everything responds before hesitation.

The product feels inevitable.

That is the standard.

---

## Things to Avoid

Do not compress desktop layouts onto mobile.

Do not use horizontal scrolling for primary navigation.

Do not place important actions at the top of the screen.

Do not use small touch targets.

Do not create cluttered screens.

Do not reduce typography to fit more content.

Do not sacrifice breathing room for information density.

Information density is not a virtue.

Clarity is.

---

## Quality Standard

The mobile experience should be indistinguishable in quality from a native application developed by a dedicated iOS team.

Every transition should feel smooth.

Every touch should feel precise.

Every screen should feel intentional.

If the mobile experience ever feels like a website,

the design has failed.

## Design Principle for Claude Code

Mobile is not a constraint.

It is a canvas.

The most considered design decisions inside Paylio happen within 430 pixels.

Every pixel counts.

Every touch matters.

Every transition is felt.

Design the mobile experience as though it will be displayed in a gallery.

Because every time a user unlocks their phone,

that is exactly where it is.
