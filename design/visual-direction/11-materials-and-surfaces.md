# PAYLIO VISUAL DIRECTION SHEET 11

# Materials & Surfaces

### The Physical Vocabulary of Paylio

---

## Purpose

Materials define how Paylio feels before users read a single word.

Before typography.

Before layout.

Before motion.

The surfaces communicate quality.

Every component should feel manufactured rather than designed.

Every surface should respond to light as though it exists physically.

Users should instinctively believe they could reach through the screen and touch something real.

---

## Emotional Objective

Materials should create:

Weight.

Permanence.

Craftsmanship.

Luxury.

Trust.

The user should feel that Paylio was built with the same precision as a finely engineered object.

Not assembled from templates.

Constructed from intention.

---

## Story

Imagine a watchmaker's studio after hours.

Soft light falls across a workbench.

Titanium components rest beside polished sapphire glass.

Ceramic housings catch edge highlights.

Every material was chosen for a reason.

Nothing was chosen for appearance alone.

The objects communicate quality through presence.

Not color.

Not decoration.

Simply through what they are made of.

That philosophy governs every surface inside Paylio.

---

## Design Philosophy

Every material should earn its place.

Glass exists to capture light.

Metal exists to communicate precision.

Ceramic exists to create softness.

OLED exists to generate warmth from within.

Nothing decorative.

Nothing arbitrary.

Materials are the honest expression of Paylio's values.

---

## The Primary Material System

### Smoked Glass

The dominant surface.

Deep.

Layered.

Slightly translucent.

Captures ambient light naturally.

Reveals depth beneath.

Never perfectly transparent.

Never completely opaque.

Glass should feel like looking into something valuable.

---

### Frosted Glass

Used for panels.

Overlays.

Navigation backgrounds.

Authentication screens.

Frosted glass softens the environment behind it.

It does not hide information.

It creates respectful distance.

---

### Brushed Titanium

Appears on edges.

Borders.

Structural elements.

Fine lines.

Brushed titanium communicates precision engineering.

Its texture is subtle.

Its highlights are controlled.

It never dominates.

It defines.

---

### Anodized Aluminium

Lighter than titanium.

Slightly warmer.

Used for secondary surfaces.

Card edges.

Button frames.

Control borders.

Anodized aluminium looks finished.

Not raw.

Not industrial.

Refined.

---

### Matte Ceramic

The quietest material.

Appears in backgrounds.

Content areas.

Areas requiring calm.

Ceramic does not reflect aggressively.

It absorbs light gently.

It creates breathing space.

It allows other materials to shine.

---

### OLED Illumination

Not a material.

A behaviour.

Every surface should feel as though it could softly illuminate from within.

Teal warmth.

Gentle bloom.

Restrained brightness.

OLED lighting is what transforms a dark surface into something alive.

---

## Material Relationships

Materials should coexist naturally.

Glass rests on ceramic.

Titanium frames glass.

OLED illuminates from beneath.

Aluminium defines structure.

Frosted panels separate foreground from background.

Each material has a role.

None competes.

---

## Depth

Paylio should never feel flat.

Every screen should have layers.

Background.

Midground.

Foreground.

Materials separate naturally across those layers.

The deepest layer carries the ceramic.

The middle layer carries the glass.

The closest layer carries the illuminated surface.

Depth is not created through shadow alone.

It is created through material logic.

---

## Reflections

Every reflective surface should behave physically.

Reflections travel naturally.

They shift with ambient lighting.

They respond to cursor movement.

They catch highlights at edges.

They never appear painted on.

Paylio reflections should look as though they were captured by a camera.

Not rendered by software.

---

## Lighting Interaction

Materials only exist fully when light touches them.

Ceramic softens light.

Glass redirects it.

Titanium catches edge highlights.

Aluminium distributes it evenly.

OLED generates it.

The material system and lighting system are inseparable.

They exist together.

---

## Atmosphere

Imagine a modern architecture firm specialising in luxury bank interiors.

They work with the same materials every time.

Stone.

Glass.

Metal.

Fabric.

But every project feels completely different.

Because materials are assembled with intention.

Paylio follows the same philosophy.

The same materials appear throughout.

But always with purpose.

Always with restraint.

Always in service of the experience.

---

## Color Through Materials

Color should not be applied.

It should emerge from materials.

Deep navy emerges from smoked glass.

Soft teal emerges from OLED illumination.

Muted violet emerges from reflected ambient light.

Pure white emerges from precise typography.

Silver emerges from titanium edges.

Color should feel discovered.

Not painted.

---

## Cinematography

Imagine photographing these materials for a luxury product catalogue.

Macro lens.

Slow shutter.

Controlled studio lighting.

Shallow depth of field.

Every surface texture visible.

Every reflection physically accurate.

Every edge catching the light precisely.

The images should make viewers want to touch the product.

---

## Things to Avoid

Do not use flat colors pretending to be materials.

Do not use fake glass gradients.

Do not use decorative textures.

Do not overuse reflections.

Do not create artificial depth.

Do not imitate Material Design.

Do not imitate glassmorphism trends.

Do not use materials without purpose.

Every surface should feel engineered.

Not styled.

---

## Quality Standard

Any single component extracted from Paylio should still feel premium in isolation.

The material quality should be visible at a glance.

Every reflection should have physical logic.

Every surface should feel touchable.

Every edge should feel manufactured.

If the materials could be fabricated in the real world,

the design is correct.

## Design Principle for Claude Code

Before specifying any color,

ask what material the surface is made from.

The material determines the light.

The light determines the color.

Color that emerges from material logic feels real.

Color that is applied on top feels designed.

Paylio should always feel real.
