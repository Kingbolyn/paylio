# PAYLIO VISUAL DIRECTION SHEET 07

# Cards

### Your Wallet, Reimagined

---

## Purpose

The Cards experience is where users interact with their financial identity.

A payment card is no longer just a way to spend money.

It is an extension of trust.

Every card should feel like a luxury object crafted with precision.

Users should enjoy opening this page even when they are not making changes.

---

## Emotional Objective

This page should create these emotions.

Ownership.

Confidence.

Elegance.

Security.

Control.

Pride.

Users should feel that they possess something premium.

Not because of the balance.

Because of the experience.

---

## Story

Imagine opening a handcrafted watch box.

Inside rests a perfectly machined timepiece.

The lighting quietly reveals polished metal, sapphire glass, and flawless craftsmanship.

Nothing moves quickly.

Nothing needs to.

The object speaks through its quality.

That is how the Paylio card should be presented.

---

## Design Philosophy

The card is the hero.

Everything else supports it.

The page is not a settings screen.

It is a product showcase.

The controls should feel secondary.

The card should command immediate attention.

---

## Visual Hierarchy

Premium Card.

↓

Available Controls.

↓

Card Status.

↓

Limits.

↓

Settings.

Nothing should compete with the card itself.

---

## Card Design

The card should become one of Paylio's signature visual assets.

Deep smoked glass.

Soft ceramic finish.

Precision-machined metal edges.

Micro-texture.

Subtle holographic reflections.

Beautiful typography.

Minimal branding.

No unnecessary decoration.

The card should look manufacturable.

Not illustrated.

---

## Card Materials

Dark sapphire glass.

Brushed titanium.

Anodized aluminium.

Frosted ceramic.

Soft OLED lighting.

Realistic reflections.

Every surface should respond naturally to light.

---

## Card Lighting

The card appears softly illuminated from above.

Gentle teal reflections move across the surface.

Muted violet highlights create depth.

Edges catch light naturally.

Nothing glows excessively.

Luxury comes from restraint.

---

## Card Motion

The card slowly floats.

Almost imperceptibly.

It gently rotates with cursor movement.

Light travels across its surface.

Reflections shift naturally.

The card feels suspended in space.

Like a museum exhibit.

Never exaggerated.

Always believable.

---

## Controls

Every control should feel intentional.

Freeze Card.

View Details.

Set Spending Limit.

Replace Card.

Rename Card.

Enable Contactless.

Each action appears as a refined glass control.

Minimal.

Elegant.

Immediate.

Users should never feel overwhelmed.

---

## Spending Limits

The limit interface should feel empowering.

Not restrictive.

If no limit exists,

display Unlimited with confidence.

If a limit is added,

the transition should feel natural.

The card quietly acknowledges the change.

---

## Card Details

Numbers should never dominate visually.

Reveal only when requested.

Sensitive information appears through elegant animation.

The user always feels protected.

Privacy is part of the experience.

---

## Security

Security controls should feel reassuring.

Not intimidating.

Every action clearly communicates what will happen.

Visual confirmation appears immediately.

The card subtly responds to successful changes.

Trust is reinforced through feedback.

---

## Atmosphere

Imagine the card displayed inside a luxury design museum.

Soft spotlights.

Dark architectural surroundings.

Quiet atmosphere.

Nothing distracts from the object.

Every angle communicates craftsmanship.

---

## Color System

Deep Navy.

Charcoal.

Graphite.

Soft Teal.

Muted Violet.

Pure White.

Brushed Silver.

Every color reinforces premium quality.

Avoid decorative gradients.

Avoid unnecessary color variation.

---

## Typography

Simple.

Confident.

Refined.

Generous spacing.

Numbers remain elegant.

Labels stay understated.

Brand identity quietly supports the experience.

---

## Motion Philosophy

Motion follows physics.

The card has weight.

Light behaves realistically.

Reflections move naturally.

Controls respond immediately.

Animations never compete for attention.

The card should always remain the visual anchor.

---

## Cinematography

Imagine filming the card for a luxury automotive commercial.

Macro photography.

Slow camera movement.

Natural reflections.

Soft shadows.

Beautiful depth.

Tiny floating dust particles.

Everything feels tangible.

The viewer should believe the card could exist physically.

---

## Things to Avoid

Do not imitate Apple Card.

Do not imitate Revolut.

Do not imitate Monzo.

Do not create excessive gradients.

Do not create flashy holograms.

Do not overload the card with branding.

Do not add unnecessary decorative textures.

Do not make the controls feel like a settings menu.

---

## Quality Standard

The Paylio card should become one of the most recognizable elements in the entire product.

It should be beautiful enough to appear in advertising by itself.

Every reflection should feel intentional.

Every material should feel premium.

Every interaction should communicate ownership.

The card should make users feel proud that it belongs to them.

## Design Principle for Claude Code

Do not think of the card as a payment method.

Think of it as the user's financial passport.

When users open this page, they should feel the same emotional response people have when they hold a finely crafted luxury object. The card is not merely functional. It reflects the quality, trust, and quiet confidence that define the entire Paylio brand.
